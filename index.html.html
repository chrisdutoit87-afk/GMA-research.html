Theme 1
Top user pain points
Each bar shows the intensity of each pain point based on frequency of mention across 55 research sources, community forums, expert analysis, and user reviews.
The maths problem
D&D 2024 removed encounter multipliers and pacing guidance. PF2e uses a creature-level XP system. Both require knowing the formula — and applying it wrong produces encounters that accidentally kill or bore players.
The lore problem
Knowing which creatures belong in which biome, faction, and environment requires years of accumulated system knowledge. No current encounter tool filters by lore — only by CR or creature level.
The map problem
Creating a tactical grid map requires a separate tool (Inkarnate, Dungeondraft) with its own learning curve and subscription. GMs who skip maps report consistently worse combat experiences.
The fragmentation problem
GMs currently switch between 3–5 tools to produce one encounter: balance calculator, monster reference, map maker, notes app. Every tool switch breaks prep flow and adds time.

Shared characteristic
All three segments are competent storytellers who are being held back by technical preparation requirements that have nothing to do with the quality of the game they want to run. The problem is not creativity — it is tooling.
Theme 3
Opportunity map — where competitors stop
The market is divided into three categories: math calculators, map makers, and VTTs. No product operates at the intersection of all three. Opportunity score = gap × market reach (1–10 scale).
The integration gap
GMs currently need 3–5 separate tools to produce one complete encounter. No product connects them. GMA is that connection — and it enters the market at the exact moment the previous category leader (Kobold Fight Club) went permanently offline.

Final recommendation
MVP direction
Integrated pre-session encounter generator
Build a single, fast, free-to-use tool that combines lore-accurate enemy generation, programmatic difficulty validation, and auto-generated tactical maps — supporting both D&D 2024 and Pathfinder 2e at launch. No account required for the first generation. The GM should be able to arrive with nothing and leave with a complete, playable encounter in under five minutes.

Why this direction is strongest
Pain points
Every pain point — maths, lore, maps, fragmentation — is solved by the integration of these three pillars in one workflow.
Trust barriers
Programmatic rules engine + lore rationale + simplicity-first UX directly addresses all four identified trust barriers.
Feature expectations
GMs want a scene (not a list), a connected map, and swap controls. All are in this direction.
Market gap
No current tool offers this combination. KFC went offline late 2024. PF2e has 1.5M new adopters with no integrated tool. The gap is real and active.
Build sequence
Recommended immediate action
Start with the PF2e rules engine. PF2e is legally safer (ORC licence), has a free complete data source (Archives of Nethys), and its encounter design system is more precisely defined. A validated PF2e encounter generator is a fully functional product for 1.5 million remastered adopters — and it builds the technical foundation that D&D 2024 support will extend.
